Nature Band


The Nature Band project seeks to explore the intersection of nature, music, and interactive design by constructing a band composed of natural elements, such as trees, flowers, and birds. Inspired by the Piano Staircase project by Fun Theory, the project aims to convey the beauty of nature through musical interactions, fostering a sense of enjoyment and engagement. Utilizing soft materials and incorporating real-life movements inspired by human-nature interactions, the project offers an immersive and educational experience for the user. The project offers a valuable exploration of the synthesis of these three realms, with the intention of imparting knowledge and appreciation for the intersection of nature, music, and design.

  • Form: Physical Installation
  • Project Type: Individual Work
  • Tools in Use: Arduino, Processing
  • Completed on: Dec. 2020




Conception & Design

The Nature Band project aims to create an interactive band featuring familiar natural elements like trees, flowers, and birds. Users can compose their own nature-inspired song through interactions with these elements, fostering a deeper appreciation for nature sounds. Inspired by the Piano Staircase project, the idea evolved to incorporate musical interactions for a meaningful and enjoyable user experience. Soft materials are used for user comfort, and real-life interactions with nature, like shaking flowers or throwing stones, are integrated to enhance the overall experience.

Fabrication & Production

Soft materials like feathers were used for the trees to enhance user enjoyment, receiving positive feedback for capturing the beauty of nature. Various sensors were employed to demonstrate diverse interactions with the natural environment, though challenges arose in managing the setup. Despite initially planning for a single, larger Arduino board, three boards were eventually used, resulting in a somewhat cluttered presentation.
