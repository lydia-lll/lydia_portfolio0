Research: Data Privacy

A research journey exploring data privacy issues in the digital age during the "New Media Research Studio" course

New Media Research Studio: Data Privacy


As part of the "New Media Research Studio," a dynamic and project-intensive course, I conducted an in-depth exploration of emerging practices and trends in new media, with a specific focus on the intersection of digital platforms and data privacy. Over the semester, I conducted four case studies on distinct platforms, addressing the privacy issues. This research culminated in a final documentary of Twitter, where each group in the class contributed a segment to study the platform from diffenret angles.

  • Form: Case Studies, Website, Documentary
  • Project Type: Individual Work & Group Project
  • Tools in Use: WordPress, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Premiere Pro
  • Completed on: May. 2022


Case Studies


Case Study #1
Displaying IP locations, Dismissing Data Privacy?

A case study into Weibo's new feature of displaying IP locations: what does the new setting mean in this age of data privacy, how does it concerns us, and why’s that important?

Case Study #2
Our Geolocation Matters: Data Privacy in Ride-Hailing Industry

A case study into DiDi, who Fined $1.2 billion for violating China cybersecurity laws: what’s the gold in our data that fascinated the ride-hailing companies so much, and why it drives China such a clampdown on Didi and data security?

Case Study #3
You Want a Cookie? Data Privacy in the Retail Industry

A case study into Walmart's big data technology and its privacy issues – after all, both our privacy and wallets can be fragile in this age of big data.

Case Study #4
Chinese Health Code System in the 'Black Box'

A case study into the data breach of Shanghai Health Code 'Sui-Shen-Ma' -- No one was found responsible for the leak, because no one really knows the whole picture of the entire system.

Final Documentary

The research project culminated in a final documentary on Twitter and its data privacy. In collaboration with my peers, our group played a key role in investigating and presenting the Terms of Service segment of the documentary. Through street interviews, we gathered diverse user experiences. We further supplemented our insights with in-depth discussions with legal and technical experts to provide a comprehensive exploration of the platform's terms and user acceptance.
