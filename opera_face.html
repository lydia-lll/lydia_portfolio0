Problem Statement

Growing up in China, we were exposed to Peking Opera on TV and in textbooks since little. Yet, one may also find that its regular listeners are comparatively rare --- especially in the younger generation.

The app aims at introducing Peking Opera to users of the new generation in a fun, engaging and accessible way.


Background

Peking Opera is a classic Chinese art form that combines music, dance, and storytelling with stunning costumes and masks to bring historical and folk tales to life

The Four Roles:

There are four primary character types in Peking Opera, each with its own distinct makeup and performance styles

Music of Peking Opera:

Instrumental resources in Peking Opera divided into two groups:

- Melodic/lyrical instruments (wen chang): accompany singing, play scene music

- Percussive/military instruments (wu chang): cooperate with actors' performance, provide rhythms


Interaction Design

The app features common percussion instruments used in Peking opera. Think of it like a face band, where different facial expressions trigger different instruments. Raise your eyebrows for the sound of a large gong; look up and hear the cymbals; look down and play the small gong; smile and hear the clappers; puff out your cheeks and hear the Peking opera drum. The louder you twist your face, the louder the instruments play.

The app also has four face filters that represent the four main roles of Peking opera. Nod your head to switch between the filters and immerse yourself in the opera. You can even record your compositions so that you can either incorporate traditional Peking opera percussion into your own music or compose a new piece of percussion music. And, check out brief introductions to each instrument and role by hitting the icons!

Tech Implementation

Technology plays a vital role in the app's immersive experience. I leveraged the power of ARkit and Scenekit to recognize users' facial expressions and trigger corresponding instrument sounds - a whole new level of interactivity and creativity to music-making! Plus, with AVFoundation, users can easily record their musical masterpieces and sing along. And to make the app even more flexible, I fully implemented it in swiftUI, using the MVC design pattern (maybe not that typical, but I tried my best as a newbiew in software architecture).
